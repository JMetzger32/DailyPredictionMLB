# C1 — Live-resolve contamination check on the 1,212-pick calibration diagnostic

Generated by `scripts/calibration_contamination_check.py` on integrate/full-fix.

## Question
Before the immutability fix, `/api/predictions` could score a stored pick using a fresh
view-time prediction. How many of the 1,212 clean 2026 entries behind the earlier live
calibration diagnostic (ECE ~0.0795) were actually affected?

## Signals tested
- **S1 pick/prob inconsistency** — `predicted_winner` disagrees with `home_win_prob`
  (Home with p<0.5 or Away with p>0.5): a rewrite of one but not the other.
- **S2 scoring inconsistency** — stored `brier_score` ≠ `(home_win_prob − actual)²`
  recomputed from the same entry (tol 2e-3): scoring came from a different probability.

## Result: ZERO contaminated entries
| signal | count | % of 1,212 |
|---|---|---|
| S1 pick/prob inconsistent | **0** | 0.0% |
| S2 brier ≠ f(stored prob) | **0** | 0.0% |
| Flagged union | **0** | 0.0% |

Coverage caveat: S2 is only testable on the 101 entries (8.3%) that carry a stored
`brier_score`; the other 1,111 predate that field. S1 covers all 1,212 and found none.

## Corrections to earlier claims (flagging our own audit)
1. **IMPROVEMENTS.md deferred-risk item 5 was wrong.** The "entries with predicted_winner
   inconsistent with home_win_prob" it described do not exist (0/1,212). The sub-50% bins in
   the earlier live-2026 table (calibration_comparison.md, table D) came from that table
   binning RAW home-win probability (so confident *away* picks land in low bins) — a
   methodology artifact, not data corruption. Confirmed: 0 entries have p<0.5 with pick=Home.
2. The earlier "ECE 0.0795" used home-prob binning; the winner-prob binning used here gives
   **0.0786** on the identical 1,212 entries. Same story, slightly different bin geometry.

## Verdict
**The over-confidence finding fully holds on an honestly-clean sample.** No live-resolve
rewrites are detectable in the log; the live model's poor calibration (60-70% picks winning
53.8%, ECE ≈ 0.079) is a genuine model property — reinforcing that the leak-fixed retrain +
(future) recalibration are the right levers, not data cleanup.

Why zero despite the bug existing: daily jobs (`update_yesterday_results`, auto-heal, the
30-min resolver) score stored picks correctly and resolved almost everything before any
mid-view live-resolve could; and when live-resolve did fire same-day, the in-memory model
and baselines were typically identical to those that logged the 5 AM pick, so the "fresh"
prediction matched the stored one.
