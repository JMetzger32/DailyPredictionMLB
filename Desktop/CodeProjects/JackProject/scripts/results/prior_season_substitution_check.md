Generated by `scripts/prior_season_substitution_check.py`. Granularity = `tgl` rows

## League-average fallback rate
A row falls back to league-average when the pitcher has no `(pid, season-1)` row in
`pitcher_stats` (debut/limited pitchers, and all of 2021 since the table starts at 2021).

**Overall: 11,260 / 24,296 rows = 46.3%.**

| season | rows | fallback | pct |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2021 | 4858 | 4858 | **100.0%** |
| 2022 | 4860 | 1718 | 35.3% |
| 2023 | 4860 | 1741 | 35.8% |
| 2024 | 4858 | 1779 | 36.6% |
| 2025 | 4860 | 1164 | 24.0% |

## sp_era distribution: OLD (same-season) vs NEW (prior-season)
| variant | mean | median | std |
|---|---|---|---|
| OLD (same-season) | 4.180 | 4.150 | 1.130 |
| NEW (prior-season) | 4.206 | 4.361 | 0.890 |
| delta (NEW − OLD) | +0.027 | +0.211 | **−0.240** |

## Interpretation
- **2021 carries zero pitcher-specific SP signal** (100% league-avg) because there is no 2020
  data. 2022–2024 lose ~35% of rows to fallback; 2025 the least (24%).
- The NEW feature's **std falls 1.130 → 0.890 (−21%)**: ~46% of rows become the league-average
  constant and prior-season stats regress toward the mean, so the SP-ERA signal is materially
  diluted/compressed in training. This is the expected, honest cost of removing look-ahead
  leakage, and is consistent with the ~0.032 AUC that Step-1/Phase-4b attribute to the leak.
- Mean is nearly unchanged (+0.027); the median rises (+0.211) because league-average fill sits
  near the center of the distribution.
- **Implication for a future retrain:** consider down-weighting or excluding 2021 (its SP feature
  is pure league-average noise), and/or a smarter prior (career-to-date SP ERA) to recover signal
  without leakage. Not done here — flagged for the retrain step.
